About This Course

The Certified HR Business Partner 2.0 is a comprehensive programme designed to equip HR professionals with the strategic capabilities required to operate as true business partners within their organisations. This course goes beyond traditional HR fundamentals and dives deep into the competencies that differentiate exceptional HRBPs from the rest.

Through a blend of theoretical frameworks, real-world case studies, and practical exercises, you will develop the skills to influence senior stakeholders, drive organisational change, and align HR strategy with business objectives. Whether you are aspiring to become an HRBP or looking to elevate your existing capabilities, this course provides the knowledge and tools you need.

This updated 2.0 version includes new modules on data-driven HR, workforce analytics, and the evolving role of HR in hybrid and remote work environments.

What You Will Learn

  • Understand the strategic role of an HR Business Partner in modern organisations
  • Develop workforce planning capabilities using data and analytics
  • Build influence and credibility with senior stakeholders
  • Design and implement effective talent management strategies
  • Lead organisational change and transformation initiatives
  • Apply HR analytics to drive evidence-based decision making
  • Navigate the complexities of hybrid and remote workforce management
  • Create HR strategies that directly support business goals

Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, you will be able to:

  • Confidently operate as a strategic HR Business Partner
  • Apply workforce planning methodologies to real business scenarios
  • Use HR analytics tools and techniques to inform decision-making
  • Build and maintain productive relationships with key stakeholders
  • Design talent strategies that attract, develop, and retain top talent
  • Lead and manage complex organisational change programmes
  • Demonstrate the business value of HR initiatives through measurable outcomes
  • Adapt HR practices for hybrid and remote working environments
  • Develop a personal action plan for continuous professional development

Requirements

  • A minimum of 2 years experience in an HR-related role
  • Basic understanding of HR functions and processes
  • Familiarity with business strategy concepts
  • Willingness to engage in self-reflection and practical exercises
  • Access to a computer with a stable internet connection
